Chapter 02Hiragana — Your First Script (ひらがな)

Memory Technique Spotlight: Method of Loci + Keyword Mnemonics

Each hiragana character gets a specific spot in your Kitchen memory palace. Combined with keyword bridges (English/Korean words that sound like the character), you'll build two independent retrieval pathways for every character.

Palace Assignment: The Kitchen (부엌)

Your kitchen becomes the home of all 46 hiragana. Vowels on the refrigerator, か-row on the counter, さ-row at the sink, た-row on the stove, な-row by the pots, は-row at the rice cooker, ま-row by the microwave, や-row at the window, ら-row at the table, わ-row + ん at the door.

Hiragana is the foundation of Japanese writing. 46 basic characters, each representing one syllable. Unlike Hangeul's systematic design, hiragana evolved from cursive kanji — so the shapes are arbitrary. You'll need mnemonics. Target: 3–7 days.

Korean Advantage

Japanese has only 5 vowels (아, 이, 우, 에, 오) and no 받침. Every syllable is open: consonant + vowel. You're downshifting from Korean's 21 vowels and complex codas. This is easier, not harder.

The Five Vowels — あ い う え お

Master these first. Every other row is just a consonant + these five vowels.

a — like 아 in Korean
Korean approximation: 아
Mnemonic: An acrobat doing a flip — see the cross-strokes forming an "A" shape. The curved stroke at the bottom is the acrobat's body mid-flip.
あ
i — like 이 in Korean
Korean approximation: 이
Mnemonic: Two eels swimming side by side. The two vertical strokes look like a pair of eels — "ee" = い.
い
u — like 우 BUT unrounded
Korean approximation: 우 (but don't round your lips!)
Mnemonic: A winding path going up. The stroke curves upward like a mountain trail.
う
Trap: Korean う

Korean 우 is pronounced with rounded lips. Japanese う is unrounded — keep your lips relaxed and slightly spread. This is Trap #2 from Ch04. Practice now: say "우" but without puckering.

e — like 에 in Korean
Korean approximation: 에
Mnemonic: An energetic person waving — the character looks like a stick figure with arms out saying "Hey!"
え
o — like 오 in Korean
Korean approximation: 오
Mnemonic: A person saying "Oh!" in surprise — see the face with an open mouth. Don't mix up with あ!
お

K-Row — か き く け こ

Add the "k" consonant to each vowel. Korean parallel: ㄱ sound (but lighter — no aspiration).

か
ka
Korean: 가 (but less aspirated)
Mnemonic: A katana (sword) cutting through. The vertical stroke is the blade, the horizontal is the guard. Practice word: かさ (kasa) = umbrella.
き
ki
Korean: 기
Mnemonic: A key — the top strokes form the key's teeth. Practice word: きく (kiku) = chrysanthemum / to listen.
く
ku
Korean: 구
Mnemonic: A cookoo bird's beak — just one angled stroke, like a beak opening. Practice word: くち (kuchi) = mouth.
け
ke
Korean: 게/케
Mnemonic: A keg of beer on its side. The vertical line is the tap. Practice word: けさ (kesa) = this morning.
こ
ko
Korean: 고/코
Mnemonic: Two corners of a corner — like an equal sign bent. Practice word: ここ (koko) = here.

さ
sa
Korean: 사
Mnemonic: A samurai slashing with a sword. Practice word: さくら (sakura) = cherry blossom.
し
shi
Korean: 시
Mnemonic: A fish hook — you're fishing in the sea (shi sounds like "she"). Practice word: しお (shio) = salt.
す
su
Korean: 수
Mnemonic: A sushi roll being picked up with a loop. Practice word: すし (sushi) = sushi.
せ
se
Korean: 세
Mnemonic: A set of shelves — horizontal strokes stacked. Practice word: せかい (sekai) = world.
そ
so
Korean: 소
Mnemonic: A zigzag — so many turns! Like thread being sown. Practice word: そら (sora) = sky.

T-Row — た ち つ て と

た
ta
Korean: 다/타
Mnemonic: Looks like "た" has a cross like the letter "t" + "a". Practice word: たべる (taberu) = to eat.
ち
chi
Korean: 치
Mnemonic: A cheerleader doing a pose — arm up, leg kicked. Practice word: ちかい (chikai) = near.
つ
tsu
Korean: 쓰 (closest, but different)
Mnemonic: A tsunami wave — one big curving wave. Practice word: つき (tsuki) = moon.
Trap: つ (tsu)

This sound doesn't exist in Korean. It's NOT just "스" or "추". Place your tongue behind your upper teeth (like ㄷ position) and release into "su". Practice: つ つ つ.

て
te
Korean: 데/테
Mnemonic: The telephone receiver — a horizontal stroke with a curved hook, like picking up a phone. Practice word: てがみ (tegami) = letter.
と
to
Korean: 도/토
Mnemonic: A toe stubbing on a door — vertical line (door) with a foot sticking out. Practice word: とり (tori) = bird.

N-Row — な に ぬ ね の

な
na
Korean: 나
Mnemonic: A naughty knot — crossed lines with a loop. Practice word: なまえ (namae) = name.
に
ni
Korean: 니
Mnemonic: A knee — the left stroke is the leg, the two right strokes are the kneecap. Practice word: にほん (nihon) = Japan.
ぬ
nu
Korean: 누
Mnemonic: Noodles tangled in a loop. Practice word: いぬ (inu) = dog.
ね
ne
Korean: 네
Mnemonic: A nell (snail) with a spiral shell. Practice word: ねこ (neko) = cat.
の
no
Korean: 노
Mnemonic: A "NO" sign — a circle with a diagonal, like a prohibition sign. Also: の = possessive particle (like 의). Practice word: のむ (nomu) = to drink.

H-Row — は ひ ふ へ ほ

は
ha (but "wa" as particle)
Korean: 하
Mnemonic: A person saying "Ha!" — laughing so hard they bend over. Key fact: as a particle, は is pronounced "wa" (like Korean 는). Practice word: はな (hana) = flower / nose.
ひ
hi
Korean: 히
Mnemonic: A person saying "hee hee" laughing — the curve is a smile. Practice word: ひと (hito) = person.
ふ
fu (between "fu" and "hu")
Korean: closest to 후 but lips don't touch
Mnemonic: Mount Fuji — the strokes form a mountain peak. Practice word: ふね (fune) = ship.
Trap: ふ (fu)

Japanese ふ is NOT English "f" or Korean "후". It's a bilabial fricative — blow air gently through both lips without them touching. Like blowing out a candle very softly. No teeth involved!

へ
he (but "e" as particle)
Korean: 헤
Mnemonic: A hill — going heading toward a destination. As a particle (direction), pronounced "e". Practice word: へや (heya) = room.
ほ
ho
Korean: 호
Mnemonic: A holy cross with a curve — like a person praying. Practice word: ほん (hon) = book.

ma
Korean: 마

み
mi
Korean: 미
Mnemonic: The number 21 — looks like "2" and "1" squished, sounds like "me." Practice: みみ (mimi) = ear.
む
mu

Mnemonic: A mooing cow's face — the loop is the nose ring. Practice: むし (mushi) = insect.

me

mo

R-Row — ら り る れ ろ

ら
ra
Korean: 라
Mnemonic: Like the number 5 written quickly. Practice: さくら (sakura) = cherry blossom.
り
ri
Korean: 리
Mnemonic: Two rivers flowing down. Practice: とり (tori) = bird.
る
ru
Korean: 루
Mnemonic: A loop at the bottom — like a ruler curling. Practice: たべる (taberu) = to eat.
れ
re
Korean: 레
Mnemonic: A reindeer's antler — the curve branches out. Practice: これ (kore) = this.
ろ
ro
Korean: 로
Mnemonic: A road — simple stroke going forward. Practice: ろく (roku) = six.

W-Row — わ を + Special: ん

わ
wa
Korean: 와
Mnemonic: A water whirlpool. Practice: わたし (watashi) = I/me.
を
wo (pronounced "o")
Korean: Object marker 을/를
Mnemonic: Only used as the object particle (like 을/를). Pronounced just like お but written differently. You'll use this every single sentence.
ん
n (standalone consonant)
Korean: ㄴ받침
Mnemonic: The ONLY hiragana that's just a consonant — no vowel attached. Like Korean ㄴ at the end of a syllable. Practice: にほん (nihon) = Japan.

Dakuten (゛) and Handakuten (゜) — Voicing Marks

Add ゛ to "speak up" (voice the consonant). Add ゜ for "POP!" (p-sound, only on h-row).

Base+ ゛ (dakuten)+ ゜ (handakuten)
か (ka)が (ga)—
さ (sa)ざ (za)—
た (ta)だ (da)—
は (ha)ば (ba)ぱ (pa)
Korean Parallel

Think of dakuten like going from ㄱ→ㄲ voicing: the base sound gets "heavier." か(ka)→が(ga) is like adding voice. は(ha)→ば(ba)→ぱ(pa) gives you the full h/b/p trio.

Yoon (拗音) — Combination Sounds

Small や, ゆ, よ after an i-row character: きゃ (kya), しゅ (shu), ちょ (cho). Like Korean compound vowels (ㅑ, ㅠ, ㅛ).

+ゃ (ya)+ゅ (yu)+ょ (yo)
き (ki)きゃ (kya)きゅ (kyu)きょ (kyo)
し (shi)しゃ (sha)しゅ (shu)しょ (sho)
ち (chi)ちゃ (cha)ちゅ (chu)ちょ (cho)
に (ni)にゃ (nya)にゅ (nyu)にょ (nyo)
ひ (hi)ひゃ (hya)ひゅ (hyu)ひょ (hyo)
み (mi)みゃ (mya)みゅ (myu)みょ (myo)
り (ri)りゃ (rya)りゅ (ryu)りょ (ryo)

Long Vowels — Length Matters!

Japanese distinguishes short and long vowels. Wrong length = wrong word:

ShortLongDifference
おばさん (obasan)おばあさん (obaasan)Aunt vs. Grandmother
おじさん (ojisan)おじいさん (ojiisan)Uncle vs. Grandfather
ここ (koko)こうこう (koukou)Here vs. High school
Mnemonic: Longer vowel = Older person

おばあさん has a longer "a" and means grandmother (older). おばさん with short "a" means aunt (younger). The vowel length mirrors the age!
