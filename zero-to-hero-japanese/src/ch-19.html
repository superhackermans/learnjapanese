Chapter 19JLPT and Beyond — The Path to True Fluency

Memory Technique Spotlight: Metacognitive Strategy + Self-Regulated Learning

This chapter is about learning how to learn. Metacognition — thinking about your own thinking — is the master skill that makes all other memory techniques more effective. By understanding your strengths, weaknesses, and optimal study patterns, you become a self-regulated learner who can tackle any language challenge.

This chapter is not the end of your journey -- it is the starting point. Over the preceding 17 chapters you have studied grammar structures, kanji strategies, honorifics, reading, writing, listening, and cultural pragmatics. Now let us integrate that knowledge toward the concrete goal of the 日本語能力試験 (JLPT), and open the road to true fluency beyond the exam.

"As a Korean speaker, you are in the most advantageous position when learning Japanese. What takes English speakers 2,200 hours, you can accomplish in less than half that time. You already know half of it."

1. JLPT Structure -- The Full Map of the 日本語能力試験

The JLPT is organized into five levels, from N5 (beginner) to N1 (most advanced). Each level is divided into three test sections: 言語知識 (Vocabulary/Grammar), 読解 (Reading), and 聴解 (Listening).

LevelKanjiVocabularyGrammarExam DurationPass Rate (Overall)Korean Speaker Advantage
N5 ~100 ~800 ~50 items 105 min ~45% Very Advantageous
N4 ~300 ~1,500 ~120 items 125 min ~35% Very Advantageous
N3 ~650 ~3,500 ~200 items 140 min ~35% Advantageous
N2 ~1,000 ~6,000 ~300 items 155 min ~35% Advantageous
N1 ~2,000 ~10,000 ~400+ items 170 min ~30% Partially Advantageous
Exam Structure Details

N5-N4: 言語知識 (文字・語彙) + 言語知識 (文法) / 読解 + 聴解 = 3 sections

N3-N1: 言語知識 (文字・語彙・文法) / 読解 + 聴解 = 2 sections (the actual content is the same, but the scoring structure differs)

You must exceed the sectional cutoff (19 points / 60 points) in every section, and also meet the overall score threshold. Failing even one section means failing the entire exam.

2. N3 Passing Plan (6-9 Months)

N3 is the level that tests everyday conversation + simple reading comprehension. Thanks to Sino-vocabulary and grammar structure transfer, Korean speakers can reach this level much faster than English speakers.

Korean Speaker N3 Grammar Advantage

Roughly 70% of the grammar items above have direct Korean equivalents. ～ことにする = ~하기로 하다, ～ために = ~하기 위해, and so on. The forms are different, but you already know the concepts.

Vocabulary / Kanji Targets

Kanji Target: ~650 Characters

~300 from N4 + an additional ~350. By leveraging Sino-vocabulary transfer, the number of truly new kanji you need to memorize is around 200.

Key kanji examples:

  • 届 (届ける) = to deliver / 届 = 届출 (report/notification)
  • 現 (現在) = present / 현(現)
  • 届 (届ける), 預 (預ける), 比 (比べる)
  • 関 (関係) = relationship / 관(關)

Vocabulary Target: ~3,500 Words

You can already guess roughly ~1,500 words through Sino-vocabulary cognates. In practice, you only need to learn about ~2,000 new words.

Focus areas:

  • 日常生活 (Daily Life): housework, cooking, health
  • 社会 (Society): news, workplace, transportation
  • 感情・性格 (Emotions / Personality): adjective expansion
  • 自然 (Nature): weather, geography, seasons

3. N2 Passing Plan (12-18 Months)

N2 is the practical gateway to employment and study abroad. Most Japanese companies and universities require N2 or above. For Korean speakers, this is the level with the best return on investment.

Vocabulary / Kanji Targets

Kanji Target: ~1,000 Characters

~650 from N3 + an additional ~350. At this stage, here are the key Sino-vocabulary transfer examples:

  • 提 (提出 = submission, 提案 = proposal)
  • 制 (制度 = system, 規制 = regulation)
  • 政 (政治 = politics, 政策 = policy)
  • 論 (理論 = theory, 論文 = thesis/paper)
  • 構 (構造 = structure, 構成 = composition)

Vocabulary Target: ~6,000 Words

~2,500 Sino-vocabulary cognates + ~1,500 native Japanese words + ~500 idiomatic expressions. By category:

  • ビジネス: business/work-related vocabulary
  • ニュース: current affairs terminology
  • 学術: basic academic terminology
  • 慣用句: idiomatic expressions / set phrases

N2 Time Management Strategy

The 読解 section is where you are most likely to run out of time. Maximize your Korean speaker kanji reading advantage:

  • Grasp meaning through Sino-vocabulary chunks first (経済的影響 = economic impact -- instantly comprehensible)
  • Read the questions first, then focus only on the relevant parts of the passage
  • 情報検索 (information retrieval) questions: aim to solve within 5 minutes

4. Korean Speaker Strategies by JLPT Section

文字・語彙 -- Characters / Vocabulary Greatest Advantage

This is the section where Sino-vocabulary cognates exert their power most directly.

  • 経済 = economy, 政治 = politics, 教育 = education
  • 環境 = environment, 技術 = technology, 伝統 = tradition
  • On'yomi rules let you guess even unfamiliar words (see Ch11-11)

Strategy: Breeze through Sino-vocabulary cognates at a confirmation level, and concentrate your time on native Japanese words and false friends (see Appendix).

文法 -- Grammar Major Advantage

SOV word order, the particle system, and suffix-based conjugation are all shared. Why Korean speakers have an edge in grammar questions:

  • ～にもかかわらず = despite ~ -- you already know the concept
  • ～ざるを得ない = cannot help but ~ -- identical double-negative structure
  • Particle selection questions: choosing に/で/を/が can be solved using Korean particle intuition

Strategy: Focus on grammar forms that have no Korean equivalent (～っぽい, ～がち, etc.). For those with equivalents, you only need to memorize the form.

読解 -- Reading Major Advantage

Your kanji / Sino-vocabulary reading ability creates a decisive speed advantage.

  • Grasping meaning through kanji chunks: 国際社会における環境問題 = environmental issues in international society
  • Conjunction pattern transfer: しかし (however), したがって (therefore), 一方 (on the other hand)
  • The logical structure of passages is similar to Korean academic texts

Strategy: Build your speed-reading ability. Do not read every character -- train yourself to quickly grasp the argument through kanji keywords + conjunctions.

聴解 -- Listening Structural Advantage

Thanks to the shared SOV structure, you can predict where the conclusion will fall in a sentence.

  • Since the verb comes at the end, you must listen to the very end to determine affirmative/negative -- same as Korean
  • Identifying sentence structure through particle sounds: は, が, を, に
  • Honorific listening: your sense of Korean honorific structures helps

Strategy: 聴解 is the section where you have the least advantage. Shadowing practice is essential to get used to Japanese-specific phonology, contracted forms (～ちゃう = ～てしまう), and natural speed.

5. Beyond the JLPT -- Toward True Fluency

The JLPT only measures passive skills (reading and listening). True fluency requires active productive ability, including speaking and writing. Your journey continues even after passing N2 or N1.

没入環境 -- Creating an Immersion Environment

  • Switch your smartphone/computer language to 日本語
  • Listen to Japanese podcasts/YouTube during your commute
  • Follow Japanese accounts on social media (NHK, experts in your field of interest)
  • Write a Japanese diary -- start with just 5 sentences a day
  • Join Japanese conversation partners/groups (online: HelloTalk, italki)

漢字 -- The Long Game with Kanji

  • Use Anki for spaced repetition study -- 15 minutes daily
  • When you encounter a new kanji, connect it to its Korean Sino-vocabulary equivalent to aid memory
  • Goal: fully master all 2,136 常用漢字 (Joyo Kanji)
  • Natural kanji exposure through reading -- novels, news, manga
  • Challenge the 漢字検定 (Kanji Kentei) for systematic study

会話力 -- Maintaining and Improving Speaking

  • Practice Japanese conversation 2-3 times per week (30 min+)
  • Shadowing: repeat dialogue from dramas/news
  • Pitch accent training -- acquire natural intonation
  • Scenario-based role play: interviews, presentations, phone calls
  • Monologue practice: speak for 3 minutes in Japanese on a topic of interest

キャリア・学術 -- Career / Academic Japanese

  • Challenge the BJT (Business Japanese Proficiency Test)
  • Employment in Japan: prepare your 履歴書 (resume) and 面接 (interview)
  • Graduate school: EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission), 論文 writing
  • Develop interpretation/translation skills -- the Korean-Japanese language pair is in high demand
  • Learn specialized terminology for your field (IT, medical, legal, etc.)
