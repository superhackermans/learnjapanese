Chapter 03Katakana — The Loanword Script (カタカナ)

Memory Technique Spotlight: Dual Coding + Contrastive Encoding

You'll learn katakana BY CONTRAST with already-known hiragana — seeing both versions side by side creates a powerful contrastive encoding that makes both stronger. Each katakana also gets a spot in your Living Room palace.

Palace Assignment: The Living Room (거실)

Your living room houses all 46 katakana. Same grouping order as the kitchen but different locations — sofa, TV stand, bookshelf, coffee table, curtains, lamp, rug, window sill, plant, and door.

Katakana is the angular twin of hiragana. Same 46 sounds, but a completely different look. While hiragana is curvy and flowing, katakana is sharp and straight -- derived from pieces of kanji rather than cursive forms. It's used for foreign loanwords, emphasis, onomatopoeia, and scientific terms. Target: 3--5 days (faster than hiragana because you already know the sounds).

Korean Advantage: Konglish = Your Shortcut

Korean is packed with English loanwords (Konglish): 커피, 텔레비전, 버스, 호텔... Japanese has the exact same words written in katakana! If you can read katakana, you can instantly recognize hundreds of Japanese words you already know from Korean. This chapter gives you that superpower.

When to Use Katakana

1. Foreign loanwords: コーヒー (coffee), テレビ (TV)
2. Foreign names: ソウル (Seoul), キム (Kim)
3. Emphasis: Like CAPS or bold in English -- ダメ! (NO!)
4. Onomatopoeia: ドキドキ (heart pounding), ワクワク (excitement)
5. Scientific/technical terms: ウイルス (virus), エネルギー (energy)

The Five Vowels -- ア イ ウ エ オ

Same sounds as hiragana あ い う え お, but sharper shapes. Learn the pair together.

a -- Hiragana pair: あ
Korean approximation: 아
Mnemonic: An antenna on a rooftop -- the angular strokes look like a TV antenna. Compare with curvy あ. Loanword: アイス (aisu) = ice cream / 아이스.
ア
i -- Hiragana pair: い
Korean approximation: 이
Mnemonic: Like the letter "I" leaning -- two straight strokes, one leaning against the other like an easel. Loanword: インターネット (intaanetto) = internet / 인터넷.
イ
u -- Hiragana pair: う
Korean approximation: 우 (unrounded)
Mnemonic: A crown pointing upward -- three strokes forming a crown shape. Loanword: ウイスキー (uisukii) = whisky / 위스키.
ウ
e -- Hiragana pair: え
Korean approximation: 에
Mnemonic: An elevator -- horizontal lines at top and bottom with a vertical shaft connecting them. Looks like the capital letter "I" or an I-beam. Loanword: エレベーター (erebeetaa) = elevator / 엘리베이터.
エ
o -- Hiragana pair: お
Korean approximation: 오
Mnemonic: An opera singer with arms spread -- the cross stroke and vertical line look like someone striking a pose. Loanword: オレンジ (orenji) = orange / 오렌지.

K-Row -- カ キ ク ケ コ

The "k" consonant row. Each has a hiragana twin. Katakana versions are more angular.

カ
ka -- Hiragana pair: か
Korean: 가/카
Mnemonic: A sharp katana blade -- the angular strokes slice diagonally. Compare with curvy か. Loanword: カメラ (kamera) = camera / 카메라.
キ
ki -- Hiragana pair: き
Korean: 기/키
Mnemonic: A key with sharp teeth -- like き but without the bottom curve. Loanword: キムチ (kimuchi) = kimchi / 김치.
ク
ku -- Hiragana pair: く
Korean: 구/쿠
Mnemonic: A cool angular hook -- like く got sharpened. Loanword: クラス (kurasu) = class / 클래스.
ケ
ke -- Hiragana pair: け
Korean: 게/케
Mnemonic: A kettle with a spout -- the horizontal stroke is the handle, the diagonal is the spout. Loanword: ケーキ (keeki) = cake / 케이크.
コ
ko -- Hiragana pair: こ
Korean: 고/코
Mnemonic: A corner -- two right angles forming an open box. Like こ stacked vertically. Loanword: コーヒー (koohii) = coffee / 커피.

S-Row -- サ シ ス セ ソ

サ
sa -- Hiragana pair: さ
Korean: 사
Mnemonic: A samurai's crossed swords -- sharp diagonal strokes. Loanword: サラダ (sarada) = salad / 샐러드.
シ
shi -- Hiragana pair: し
Korean: 시
Mnemonic: A she winking -- three strokes that form a smiley face tilted sideways. The dots are the eyes, the curve is the smile. CAREFUL: Don't confuse with ツ (tsu)! シ has dots going bottom-to-top with a more horizontal curve. Loanword: シャツ (shatsu) = shirt / 셔츠.
ス
su -- Hiragana pair: す
Korean: 스
Mnemonic: A super slide -- one straight stroke angling down. Loanword: スプーン (supuun) = spoon / 스푼.
セ
se -- Hiragana pair: せ
Korean: 세
Mnemonic: A set of steps going up -- angular strokes stepping upward. Loanword: セーター (seetaa) = sweater / 스웨터.
ソ
so -- Hiragana pair: そ
Korean: 소
Mnemonic: A soft serve ice cream -- two strokes swooping down. CAREFUL: Don't confuse with ン (n)! ソ has strokes going top-to-bottom with a more vertical curve. Loanword: ソファー (sofaa) = sofa / 소파.
Danger Zone: Commonly Confused Katakana

These pairs trip up EVERY beginner. Study them carefully:

シ (shi) vs ツ (tsu): シ has dots going diagonally up (bottom-left to top-right) and a horizontal curve. ツ has dots going diagonally down (top-left to bottom-right) and a vertical curve. Think: シ is a smiling face (horizontal mouth), ツ has tears falling down (vertical drip).

ソ (so) vs ン (n): Same principle! ソ strokes sweep downward (like ツ's pattern). ン strokes sweep upward (like シ's pattern). ソ = short, sharp. ン = longer, swooping up.

ウ (u) vs ワ (wa) vs フ (fu): ウ has a flat top stroke. ワ has an open top. フ is just two strokes with no side stroke.

T-Row -- タ チ ツ テ ト

タ
ta -- Hiragana pair: た
Korean: 다/타
Mnemonic: Like a tall letter "T" with extra flair. Loanword: タクシー (takushii) = taxi / 택시.
チ
chi -- Hiragana pair: ち
Korean: 치
Mnemonic: A cheerleader doing a sharp kick -- angular version of ち. Loanword: チキン (chikin) = chicken / 치킨.
ツ
tsu -- Hiragana pair: つ
Korean: 쓰 (closest)
Mnemonic: Three drops of water during a tsunami -- dots splash down, curve sweeps vertically. REMEMBER: Dots go top-to-bottom (unlike シ). Loanword: ツアー (tsuaa) = tour / 투어.
テ
te -- Hiragana pair: て
Korean: 데/테
Mnemonic: A telephone pole -- horizontal bar on top with a straight line going down. Loanword: テレビ (terebi) = TV / 텔레비전.
ト
to -- Hiragana pair: と
Korean: 도/토
Mnemonic: A totem pole -- one vertical line with a small horizontal branch. Simplest katakana! Loanword: トイレ (toire) = toilet / 화장실.

N-Row -- ナ ニ ヌ ネ ノ

ナ
na -- Hiragana pair: な
Korean: 나
Mnemonic: A nail driven at an angle -- a cross with one diagonal stroke. Loanword: ナイフ (naifu) = knife / 나이프.
ニ
ni -- Hiragana pair: に
Korean: 니
Mnemonic: Two horizontal lines = ni (2 in Japanese)! The character literally looks like the number two. Loanword: ニュース (nyuusu) = news / 뉴스.
ヌ
nu -- Hiragana pair: ぬ
Korean: 누
Mnemonic: Noodles in a sharp bowl -- angular crossed strokes. Loanword: カヌー (kanuu) = canoe / 카누.
ネ
ne -- Hiragana pair: ね
Korean: 네
Mnemonic: A net -- angular strokes crossing like a net. Loanword: ネクタイ (nekutai) = necktie / 넥타이.
ノ
no -- Hiragana pair: の
Korean: 노
Mnemonic: A single slash -- saying "NO!" with a sharp strike. The simplest katakana: just one diagonal stroke. Loanword: ノート (nooto) = notebook / 노트.

H-Row -- ハ ヒ フ ヘ ホ

ハ
ha -- Hiragana pair: は
Korean: 하
Mnemonic: "Ha!" -- two strokes spreading out like arms thrown up in laughter. Loanword: ハンバーガー (hanbaagaa) = hamburger / 햄버거.
ヒ
hi -- Hiragana pair: ひ
Korean: 히
Mnemonic: A person saying "hee" -- looks like a smiling mouth turned sideways. Loanword: ヒーロー (hiiroo) = hero / 히어로.
フ
fu -- Hiragana pair: ふ
Korean: 후 (lips don't touch)
Mnemonic: Mount Fuji's peak -- just two strokes forming a simple mountain peak. Loanword: フライドポテト (furaidopoteto) = french fries / 감자튀김.
ヘ
he -- Hiragana pair: へ
Korean: 헤
Mnemonic: Looks exactly like hiragana へ! One of the only katakana that's identical to its hiragana partner. A simple mountain peak / roof shape. Loanword: ヘリコプター (herikoputaa) = helicopter / 헬리콥터.
ホ
ho -- Hiragana pair: ほ
Korean: 호
Mnemonic: A holy cross -- vertical stroke with horizontal bars and angled sides. Loanword: ホテル (hoteru) = hotel / 호텔.

M-Row -- マ ミ ム メ モ

マ
ma -- Hiragana pair: ま
Korean: 마
Mnemonic: A math angle -- two strokes forming a sharp angle, like a less-than sign with flair. Loanword: マクドナルド (makudonarudo) = McDonald's / 맥도날드.
ミ
mi -- Hiragana pair: み
Korean: 미
Mnemonic: Three horizontal lines = mi (3 in Japanese)! Like ニ (ni=2) but with three lines. Loanword: ミルク (miruku) = milk / 밀크.
ム
mu -- Hiragana pair: む
Korean: 무
Mnemonic: A cow's face -- the angular lines form a mooing cow. Loanword: ムード (muudo) = mood / 무드.
メ
me -- Hiragana pair: め
Korean: 메
Mnemonic: An "X" marks the spot -- like checking something off a menu. Loanword: メニュー (menyuu) = menu / 메뉴.
モ
mo -- Hiragana pair: も
Korean: 모
Mnemonic: More lines -- horizontal bars with a vertical connecting them, like adding more shelves. Loanword: モデル (moderu) = model / 모델.

Y-Row -- ヤ ユ ヨ (only 3)

ヤ
ya -- Hiragana pair: や
Korean: 야
Mnemonic: A yacht's mast -- angular strokes like a sailboat rigging. Loanword: ヤクルト (yakuruto) = Yakult / 야쿠르트.
ユ
yu -- Hiragana pair: ゆ
Korean: 유
Mnemonic: A unique hook -- like a fish hook with an extra bar. Loanword: ユニフォーム (yunifoomu) = uniform / 유니폼.
ヨ
yo -- Hiragana pair: よ
Korean: 요
Mnemonic: A yoga pose -- like the letter "E" without the bottom line, a person bending. Loanword: ヨーグルト (yooguruto) = yogurt / 요거트.

R-Row -- ラ リ ル レ ロ

Same flapped "r" as hiragana -- like Korean ㄹ between vowels.

ラ
ra -- Hiragana pair: ら
Korean: 라
Mnemonic: A rabbit ear -- angular stroke sticking up. Loanword: ラーメン (raamen) = ramen / 라면.
リ
ri -- Hiragana pair: り
Korean: 리
Mnemonic: Two rivers -- like り but sharper. Looks similar to い but the right stroke curves. Loanword: リモコン (rimokon) = remote control / 리모컨.
ル
ru -- Hiragana pair: る
Korean: 루
Mnemonic: A tree root -- two lines meeting at the bottom like roots spreading. Loanword: ルール (ruuru) = rule / 룰.
レ
re -- Hiragana pair: れ
Korean: 레
Mnemonic: A sharp relay baton -- one angular stroke going up and turning. Loanword: レストラン (resutoran) = restaurant / 레스토랑.
ロ
ro -- Hiragana pair: ろ
Korean: 로
Mnemonic: A robot's mouth -- a simple open rectangle. Easiest katakana to remember! Loanword: ロボット (robotto) = robot / 로봇.

W-Row -- ワ ヲ + Special: ン

ワ
wa -- Hiragana pair: わ
Korean: 와
Mnemonic: A wallet open at the top -- like ウ but without the top stroke. Loanword: ワイン (wain) = wine / 와인.
ヲ
wo (pronounced "o") -- Hiragana pair: を
Korean: Object marker 을/를
Mnemonic: Rarely used in modern Japanese (を is used instead). You'll almost never see ヲ except in very old texts or special contexts. Looks like フ with an extra horizontal top stroke.
ン
n (standalone consonant) -- Hiragana pair: ん
Korean: ㄴ받침
Mnemonic: The ONLY consonant-only character. CAREFUL: Don't confuse with ソ (so)! ン's strokes sweep upward (like シ), while ソ's strokes go downward (like ツ). Loanword: パン (pan) = bread / 빵.

Korean Parallel

Dakuten is like voicing in Korean consonants: ㄱ(g) / ㅋ(k) / ㄲ(kk). Japanese is simpler -- you only have two variants: base (voiceless) and dakuten (voiced). The handakuten (゜) only applies to the H-row, giving you the complete ha/ba/pa set.

Long Vowels in Katakana -- The ー Dash

In katakana, long vowels are written with a simple dash (ー) called "chouon." This is different from hiragana, which uses extra vowel characters.

Don't Forget the Dash!

The long vowel dash ー changes meaning! ビル (biru) = building, but ビール (biiru) = beer. Missing the dash is like spelling "desert" when you mean "dessert."

The Loanword Bridge: 50 Korean-Japanese Cognate Loanwords

This is your secret weapon. Both Korean and Japanese borrowed heavily from English. If you know the Korean loanword, you already know the Japanese word -- you just need to read it in katakana. Study this list and you'll instantly add 50 words to your Japanese vocabulary!

How Loanwords Transform

English words get adapted to fit each language's sound system. Korean adds 받침 and uses Korean vowels. Japanese drops consonant clusters and adds vowels after every consonant. The results are often strikingly similar!
English "coffee": Korean 커피 (keopi) / Japanese コーヒー (koohii)
English "bus": Korean 버스 (beoseu) / Japanese バス (basu)

Elaborative Interrogation

Look at the table above. Why do you think some words are almost identical in Korean and Japanese (e.g., 카메라/カメラ) while others are completely different (e.g., 축구/サッカー)? Hint: It depends on whether both languages borrowed from the same source language and at the same time.

Small ッ (Sokuon) -- Double Consonants

A small ッ before a consonant doubles that consonant. Think of it as a brief pause -- like a tiny catch in your throat.

Korean Parallel

Korean has a similar concept with 쌍자음 (double consonants) like ㄲ, ㅆ, ㅃ. The Japanese small ッ works like Korean 받침 followed by the same consonant: "카ㅂ" → "카ppu" (カップ). You already understand the tension!
